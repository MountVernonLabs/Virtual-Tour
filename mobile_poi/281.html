• Water Valves, Trough, and drains (1 of 2)

    Cool water from the mill race enters the distillery in a wooden trough. From there it flows down a chute to each worm tub. A valve controls how much water flows into each tub. During distillation a regular stream of cool water must be added to the tubs in order to have the desired effect of condensing the vapor in the tubes. Water flows from the front of each tub into drains below the floor.
  • Water Valves, Trough, and drains (2 of 2)

    On the rear of each still is another valve which allows the slop, or left over water and grain, to be removed from the stills after distillation. This material, which has very little alcohol content, was used to feed cattle and hogs. A visitor to the distillery in 1798 observed 150 pigs so fat “they can hardly drag their big bellies on the ground.”
