• Copper Pot Still (1 of 2)

    There are five copper pot stills in the distillery, just as there were in the 18th century. Fermented mash is poured into these stills, and, when heated by fire from below, the alcohol is separated from the liquid mash and rises as vapor to the top of the still. The alcohol vapor travels from the onion, or head, into the copper worm tube which is contained in a wooden worm tub. Cool water from the nearby mill race flows into the worm tub and condenses the vapor back into a liquid. This liquid, now whiskey, drips into a bucket.
