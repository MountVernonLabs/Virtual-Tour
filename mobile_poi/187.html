• Dogue Run

    The creek, Dogue Run, takes its name from the Doeg Indians, a Native American tribe located in the region prior to large-scale inhabitation by British colonists. Dogue Run was part of a vital transportation network for Washington’s 18th-century gristmill. Pole driven flat boats loaded with export goods would rendezvous with an ocean-bound vessel traveling from Alexandria to the Chesapeake Bay on the Potomac. Washington’s products, like superfine flour, were then sold at American ports of call, as well as those in England, Portugal, and the West Indies.
