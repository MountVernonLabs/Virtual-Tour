• Rope Bed

    Rope beds were common in the 18th century before the invention of metal spring mattresses. A long rope was threaded through holes in the bed frame to create a net-like foundation for the mattress. One rope went from side-to-side and the other up and down. A rope tightener could be used to increase the tension when the rope began to sag or loosen after prolonged use. Eighteenth-century mattresses were stuffed with grasses, leaves, rags, or straw. Expensive feather beds, such as those in the Mansion, were available to those who could afford them.
