• Architecture

    Washington’s greenhouse and slave quarters were reconstructed between 1950 and 1952 based on original floor plans and a 19th-century drawing of the building façade from an insurance policy. The dark patches on the wall behind and to the right of the bunks are the only bricks that survive from the original greenhouse slave quarters, built in 1791–1792, and which burned in 1835. Other bricks used also had an illustrious history, though, as a 1953 report on the project noted: “By a happy coincidence... the White House was then being rebuilt, and thousands of 18th-century handmade bricks replaced by concrete and steel. With the ready cooperation of the Commission on Renovation of the Executive Mansion, bricks from the White House were made available to the staff at Mount Vernon.”
