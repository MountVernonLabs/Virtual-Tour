• Small Room (1 of 3)

    The small bedchamber on the second floor would likely only have been occupied when the rest of the chambers were filled. The lesser status of the small room is indicated by the fact that the space has no fireplace, which would have made it one of the coldest rooms in the winter. Additionally, the narrow width of the room made it more difficult to maneuver and far less commodious than other spaces on the second floor. The room second also likely had a low-post bedstead rather than a tall-post bedstead with elaborate hangings like those in other rooms.

  • Rose Blanket (3 of 3)

    The bedstead is covered with a rose blanket reproduced from a Washington-owned example. Rose blankets began to appear in Virginia by the 1770s and there are several in the Mount Vernon collection with strong Washington provenances. Rose blankets were inexpensive and imported from England, where women were paid a few shillings per blanket to sew the abstracted rose or compass pattern in the corners.

    Gift of Mrs. Walter Gibson Peter, Jr. in memory of Agnes Peter Mott, 1975 [W-2679]
