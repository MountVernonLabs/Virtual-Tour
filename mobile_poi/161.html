• Bust of John Paul Jones (1 of 2)

    The father of the American Navy, John Paul Jones was made famous by his daring defeat of the British ship HMS Serapis with his flagship, the Bonhomme Richard in a September 1779 battle. While visiting France in 1780, he was sculpted by Jean-Antoine Houdon, the most famous sculptor in Europe. The painted plaster bust that Jones presented to Washington in 1787 was destroyed by fire in 1871. It is represented by a 20th-century replica.

  • Bonhomme Richard vs. HMS Serapis (2 of 2)

    One of the engravings, executed in 1781 by Daniel Lerpinière and James Fittler after Richard Paton, depicts the 1779 Revolutionary War battle off the eastern coast of England between British Captain Richard Pearson of the HMS Serapis and American John Paul Jones of the Bonhomme Richard. When the American vessel was struck and nearly disabled, Jones is said to have uttered the famous words, “I have not yet begun to fight.” After several more hours of fighting, the British surrendered.
