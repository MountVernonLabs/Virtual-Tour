• Weathervane

    While presiding over the Constitutional Convention during the summer of 1787, George Washington commissioned master builder Joseph Rakestraw to construct this weathervane for Mount Vernon’s cupola. Washington determined the ornament's dove-of-peace design, and instructed his nephew George Augustine Washington, to paint "the bill of the bird… black, and the Olive branch in its mouth… green." Drawing on classical iconography, Washington's weathervane symbolized his hopes for domestic peace as he and the delegates in Philadelphia charted the course of the new nation.

    Transferred to the Mount Vernon Ladies' Association through the generosity of John Augustine Washington III, 1860 [W-2492]
