• Acts of Congress (1 of 3)

    George Washington's 1789 copy of the Acts passed at a Congress of the United States of America contains key founding documents establishing the Union: the Constitution, the Bill of Rights, and a record of acts passed by the first Congress. In the margins of four separate pages, Washington wrote the words "President," "Powers," and "Required," underscoring the responsibilities of the first Chief Executive.

  • Acts passed at a Congress of the United States of America (2 of 3)

    Specially bound for Washington with detailed tooling and label on the cover, “PRESIDENT. OF THE UNITED STATES.” A similarly bound copy was presented by Washington to Chief Justice John Jay, and another was owned by Thomas Jefferson, then Secretary of State.

  • Acts passed at a Congress of the United States of America (3 of 3)

    Washington marked two paragraphs in the text concerning the president’s power to veto acts passed by Congress. He wrote the word, “President” three times, highlighting his understanding of the executive’s role in the Constitution’s system of checks and balances between the different branches of government.
