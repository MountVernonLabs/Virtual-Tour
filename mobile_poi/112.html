• Mirrors and Brackets (1 of 2)

    This mirror and bracket may have been among the “Brackets glasses etc” that Washington purchased on March 16, 1791, from Philadelphia carver James Reynolds. They likely graced the Green Drawing Room of the presidential mansion in Philadelphia, and Washington subsequently brought one pair home to grace the walls of his New Room.

    Mirror: Purchased with funds donated by Mrs. Albert Harkness, Regent and Vice Regent for Rhode Island, in memory of her husband, Thomas I. Powel, 1940 [W-1181/A]; Bracket: Gift of George Washington Custis Lee, 1908 [W-57/B]

  • Mirrors and Brackets (2 of 2)

    The bracket or shelf supported a “lustre” with “two lights,” or a candelabra with two arms. The lustrous reflections of the gilded ensemble illuminated evening entertainments, including the Friday “drawing rooms” hosted by Mrs. Washington during the presidency.
