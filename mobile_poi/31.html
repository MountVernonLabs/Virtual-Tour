• Washington before the Battle of Trenton (1 of 2)

    This 1796 stipple engraving depicts a calm and resolute Washington before the Battle of Trenton, in which the American troops defeated a garrison of Hessian soldiers after the famous Christmas night crossing of the Delaware River in December of 1776.

    Purchased with funds provided by Steven Tian, 2011 [M-5262]
  • Trumbull’s Portrait (2 of 2)

    English engraver Thomas Cheesman executed the engraved portrait after John Trumbull’s 1792 full-length painting, widely considered one of the best depictions of Washington. Trumbull wrote that he intended “to give his military character, in the most sublime moment of its execution.”
