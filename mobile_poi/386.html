• Wallpaper Discovery (1 of 2)

    Investigations into the stability of the east wall of the room uncovered small fragments of early plaster surface bearing wallpaper. Analysis of related paint evidence on the mantel indicated that this early plaster was the original 1758 plaster and wallpaper that had been covered by repairs conducted in the 1760s.
  • Wallpaper Discovery (2 of 2)

    This early wallpaper fragment features a deep blue green made from the pigment verditer, and black outlines from a block-printed design. The sinuous curves of the black outlines, likely representing leaves or petals, link it to the floral designs found on English chintz papers c. 1750-1770. The fragment confirmed that this room received one of the “Chintz” or “India-figurd” papers that George Washington acquired in 1758.
