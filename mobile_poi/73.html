• Story: Preserving the Mansion (1 of 2)

    One of the first tasks facing the Mount Vernon Ladies' Association was the restoration of the Mansion, which had fallen into disrepair. A group of visitors in May 1860 described the state of the Mansion: "Some fifty rods from the tomb is the house of Washington; this is now in a dilapidated condition; the broad portico in front, is temporarily propped up to keep it from falling; even the stone floor under the portico gives evidence of crumbling back to dust."
  • The Ladies of Mount Vernon (2 of 2)

    Restoration began immediately and the estate was opened to the public. Since that time, the Mount Vernon Ladies' Association has owned and maintained Mount Vernon under a charter granted by the Commonwealth of Virginia. The Association was the first national historic preservation organization and is the oldest women's patriotic society in the United States. Its pioneering efforts in the field of preservation set an important precedent and have served as a model for many subsequent endeavors.
