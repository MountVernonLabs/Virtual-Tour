• Small Family Pastels (1 of 3)

    The display of family portraits in the west parlor included five small pastels, drawn about 1796. In addition to the president and Mrs. Washington, the English artist James Sharples depicted three younger members of the household. Seen here is George Washington Lafayette (1779–1849), the son of the president’s friend, the Marquis de Lafayette. The younger Lafayette spent several months with the Washingtons when his own father was imprisoned during the French Revolution. A facsimile of the pastel is in the west parlor today.

  • Nelly Parke Custis (2 of 3)

    Depicted at about age 17 in this 1796 pastel portrait by James Sharples, Eleanor (Nelly) Parke Custis (1779–1852) was the third of Martha Washington’s four grandchildren and the youngest granddaughter. She was considered both beautiful and accomplished, excelling in the subjects considered appropriate for well-bred young ladies, including music, drawing, embroidery, French, and dancing. A facsimile of the pastel is in the west parlor today.

  • George Washington Parke Custis (3 of 3)

    George Washington (Washy) Parke Custis (1781–1857) was Martha Washington’s youngest grandchild and only grandson. After the death of his father, John Parke Custis, in 1781, Washy and his sister Nelly were raised by George and Martha Washington. The two youngsters resided in the president’s households in both New York and Philadelphia, where this portrait was drawn by James Sharples about 1796. A facsimile of the pastel is in the west parlor today.
