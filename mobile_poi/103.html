• Palladian Window (1 of 2)

    The Palladian window on the north end of the Mansion is one of the house's most distinctive features. The window illuminates the New Room planned by George Washington as part of his second wave of renovations to the house, completed in 1787. Made of a large quantity of imported glass and providing a generous view of the Potomac River plantation, the almost 14-foot high Palladian window is a centerpiece of Mount Vernon’s most elaborate room.
