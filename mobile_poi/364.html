• Commander-in-Chief Flag

    The large blue flag with 13 six-pointed stars at the entrance to the Tomb is a stylized replica of George Washington’s Commander-in-Chief Flag. Throughout the Revolutionary War, Washington’s Life Guard carried a flag of similar elements and posted it outside Washington’s headquarters and any other place he entered. The flag symbolized Washington’s presence, authority, and steadfast commitment to the cause of liberty and the American nation. Washington’s original Commander-in-Chief flag is preserved at the Valley Forge Historical Society.
