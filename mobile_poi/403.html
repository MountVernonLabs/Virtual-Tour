• What did Field Workers Do? (1 of 4)

    The work of field hands varied with the season. Almost every day brought hard manual labor, sometimes in bitter cold, driving rain, or sweltering heat.

    • Spring: Sowing wheat and timothy; planting corn, peas, and potatoes; hauling and scattering dung; hoeing and harrowing fields

    Based on records for Mount Vernon’s River Farm in 1786
  • What did Field Workers Do? (2 of 4)

    • Summer: Planting cabbage, corn, and turnips; weeding peas; plowing; cutting, threshing, and carting rye, oats, and wheat

    Based on records for Mount Vernon’s River Farm in 1786
  • What did Field Workers Do? (3 of 4)

    • Fall: Picking apples and pressing cider; planting flax and wheat; picking peas; picking, husking, and shelling corn; digging and carting potatoes; cutting straw; leveling ground; hauling wood

    Based on records for Mount Vernon’s River Farm in 1786
  • What did Field Workers Do? (4 of 4)

    • Winter: slaughtering hogs and salting meat; clearing ground; building fences and animal pens; cutting and hauling hay and straw; making baskets; threshing wheat; clearing swamps

    Based on records for Mount Vernon’s River Farm in 1786
