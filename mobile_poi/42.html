• Mantel Clock (1 of 4)

    This fashionable Louis XVI-style mantel clock is one of many Parisian items that the Washingtons acquired after the American Revolution opened up trade with France. It is a fanciful concoction, with stone base, ebonized wood columns, and gilded brass, or ormolu, fittings. Supporting the pagoda-like finial are the heads of two griffins, a mythical creature that appeared as the crest in George Washington’s coat of arms.

    Gift of Mrs. George W. Woodward, Vice Regent for Kentucky, 1890 [W-11]
    Image credit: Hal Conroy
  • Griffin Heads (2 of 4)

    Stylized griffins’ heads arch protectively over the face of the clock, enacting their traditional role as guardians of treasure. With the head of an eagle on the body of a lion, the mythical griffin was a proud and fierce creature. Figuratively combining the king of beasts with the king of birds, griffins symbolize strength and military leadership, qualities of particular significance to George Washington.

    Image credit: Hal Conroy
  • Clock Pendulum (3 of 4)

    The clock’s pendulum displays the radiant head of a classical cherub, or perhaps the sun god, Apollo.

    Image credit: Edward Owen
  • Keeping Time at Mount Vernon (4 of 4)

    Although George Washington owned several watches and was noted for his punctuality, his inventory lists only one clock: “A Time Piece” in the Washingtons’ bedchamber with the high value of $100, thought to be the French mantel clock that the president acquired in the early 1790s. Surprisingly, there is no record of Washington’s owning a tall case clock, such as that previously displayed on Mount Vernon’s stairway landing.
