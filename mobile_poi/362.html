• Overview

    George Washington died on December 14, 1799, and was buried in the old family tomb. Before his death, Washington surveyed a location for a new tomb and he left specific instructions for his burial in his will: “The family Vault at Mount Vernon requiring repairs, and being improperly situated besides, I desire that a new one of Brick, and upon a larger Scale, may be built at the foot of what is commonly called the Vineyard Inclosure.” However, the remains of George Washington and other family members were not moved to this “New Tomb” until 1831, when the first stage of construction was completed.
