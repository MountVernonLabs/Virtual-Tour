• Wallpaper (1 of 2)

    This reproduction wallpaper features a “sprig” pattern, so called because of the use of the small floral motifs throughout. In the 1790s, sprig pattern papers were thought best for use in bedchambers.
  • Green Verditer Fragment (2 of 2)

    This small fragment of wallpaper was found beneath the floorboards in this room, and provided a clue to one of the papers the Washingtons may have used in the room. The ground of the paper is painted in a bright green verditer, a slightly diluted version of the color found on the New Room paper. This color was used as the ground for the reproduction paper installed in the room.
