• Chintz Bed (1 of 3)

    While the original bedhangings from this room did not survive, the room name provided a strong clue to its original appearance. In the eighteenth-century, Chintz Rooms typically had a chintz-hung bedstead as their centerpiece. Chintz referred to Indian cottons with bold floral designs as well as the less-expensive English imitations of them. This reproduction is based on an English chintz design.

    Purchase, 2015 [D-50.1-14]
  • Source textile (2 of 3)

    This original chintz fabric, c. 1770-1780, in the collection of Winterthur museum, provided the inspiration for the reproduction hangings. Many of the once vivid colors had faded in the original, particularly the purples, yellows, and greens, and were recreated in the reproduction.
  • GW purchase of bed furniture (3 of 3)

    In the fall of 1774, George Washington purchased three sets of “bed furniture” from the up-and-coming upholstery firm run by John and Betsy Ross in Philadelphia. He likely installed one of these sets of hangings here in the Chintz Room.
