• Story: Sinking of the Federalist

    The Federalist was a tiny, 15-foot ship and the centerpiece of Baltimore’s celebration of Maryland’s ratification of the U.S. Constitution on April 28, 1788. Joshua Barney, the ship’s designer and a prominent naval officer, sailed the ship to Mount Vernon and presented it to George Washington as a gift from the merchants of Baltimore on June 8, 1788. Unfortunately, the Federalist, as recorded in Washington’s diary, was sunk in a violent storm that hit Mount Vernon just six weeks later on July 24, 1788. Underwater archaeological efforts begun in 2010 have failed to identify remains of this historic wreck.
