• The Battle at Bunker’s Hill (1 of 3)

    The dramatized scene in this engraving depicts the death of General Joseph Warren in one of the early battles of the American Revolution. George Washington subscribed to four copies of this engraving and a companion print, The Death of General Montgomery in the Attack of Quebec, and hung two of each in the New Room. The Battle at Bunker’s Hill near Boston was engraved by Johann Gotthard von Müller after John Trumbull in 1798.

  • African American Soldier (2 of 3)

    The African American man in the lower right-hand corner may represent Peter Salem, a former slave who is credited with killing British Major John Pitcairn during the battle. Salem was one of more than 5,000 black soldiers, both free and enslaved, who fought for the Continental Army.

  • Painting of The Battle of Bunker’s Hill (3 of 3)

    John Trumbull commissioned the engraving from his original 1786 history painting. As an aide to General Washington, the young Trumbull had witnessed the Battle of Bunker Hill from the American encampment at nearby Roxbury, Massachusetts. He went on to paint a number of scenes from the revolutionary conflict, four of which currently hang in the U. S. Capitol Rotunda.
