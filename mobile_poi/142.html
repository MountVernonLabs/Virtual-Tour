• Thomas Law (1 of 2)

    This handsome gentleman is Thomas Law (1759–1834), a well-born Englishman who married Elizabeth (Eliza) Parke Custis (1776–1831), Martha Washington’s oldest grandchild. The portrait was painted by Gilbert Stuart circa 1796, the year of the couple’s marriage, and about the same time as he also painted Eliza Custis and George Washington. It hung in Mount Vernon’s west parlor in 1799 and was subsequently handed down in the Law family.

    Gift of Katherine Merle-Smith Thomas, 2010 [W-2909]

  • Elizabeth Parke Custis (2 of 2)

    This portrait depicts Elizabeth (Eliza) Parke Custis at age 19, shortly before her marriage to the wealthy Englishman Thomas Law. This canvas is a mid-19th-century copy attributed to Anna Claypoole Peale; the original portrait was painted by Gilbert Stuart in 1796, at the same time he was painting the portrait of George Washington that today appears on the dollar bill.

    Purchased with funds donated by Melody Sawyer Richardson, Vice Regent for Ohio, 2008 [H-4833]
