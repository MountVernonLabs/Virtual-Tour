• Famous visitors (1 of 3)

    In 1824, the Marquis de Lafayette came to pay respects to his Commander-in-Chief and surrogate father. Thomas Edison had his picture taken in front of Washington’s tomb in 1916. The tomb has also been visited by several presidents, members of Congress, foreign dignitaries, and royalty.
  • Royal visits, past and present (2 of 3)

    Over the years, numerous royal guests to Mount Vernon have paid their respects at George Washington’s tomb. Several members of the British royal family have visited Mount Vernon and the tomb, the first in 1860, when Prince Albert planted a tree near the tomb in Washington’s honor. During the 20th century, Queen Elizabeth II visited in 1951 and 1991. Most recently, Prince Charles and the Duchess of Cornwall participated in a ceremony and laid a wreath at Washington’s tomb on March 18, 2015.

    Prince Charles, Governor McAuliffe, and Regent Barbara Lucas at the New Tomb in 2015.
  • FDR and Churchill (3 of 3)

    President Franklin Delano Roosevelt made many visits to Mount Vernon. The most noteworthy visit took place in the midst of World War II, on January 8, 1942. President Roosevelt was accompanied by British Prime Minister Winston Churchill, and the two world leaders laid a wreath at Washington’s tomb and visited the Mansion, both signing the guest book.
