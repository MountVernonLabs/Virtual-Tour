• Eastern red columbine (Aquilegia canadensis)

    This beautiful native perennial of North America grows well in woodland and rocky gardens. Columbine grows 2 to 3 feet tall and 1 to 1.5 feet wide. It flowers from April to May, providing nectar for butterflies and hummingbirds. It has been cultivated in Europe and America since the mid-1600s. In 1625, Francis Bacon, an Englishman, wrote an essay about gardening, noting columbine blooming in May and June.
