• Millrace

    The millrace provides flowing water to turn the gristmill’s large waterwheel. In the 18th century, the millrace was fed from a millpond two miles away. The millpond sat at the crest of a hill and the water flowed downhill through a race that was 6 feet wide and approximately 7 to 8 feet deep. While this was a simple system to power Washington’s mill, drought, flooding, and icy conditions were a persistent problem. Per Washington’s directives, if there was both rain and grain, the gristmill might run 24 hours a day.
