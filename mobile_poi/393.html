• Gardener’s Report, January 12, 1793

    This slip of paper records the work of four enslaved men supervised by Mount Vernon’s hired German gardener. In the previous week, the laborers had been “digging and planting the Cuttings of the Weeping Willow,” and “planting fruit trees & leveling the Gravel Walk,” among other tasks. Reports like this from across the estate were compiled by the plantation manager into a single weekly report for Washington’s perusal.

    Possibly written by John Christian Ehlers, January 12, 1793

    Gift of Mrs. DeCourcy W. Thom, 1944
