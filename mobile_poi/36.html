• Jug (1 of 2)

    Chinese export jugs or guglets (pronounced “juglets”) and wash basins were frequently found in elite colonial American residences. George Washington's estate inventory lists them in nearly every bedchamber. This guglet, with its dramatic dragon handle, descended in the family of Martha Washington’s granddaughter, Eleanor (Nelly) Parke Custis Lewis.

    Gift: Jess and Grace Pavey Fund, 2004 [W-4360]
  • Wash Basin (2 of 2)

    Enslaved housemaids, such as Caroline or Molly, would have filled a guglet with fresh water and emptied the dirty liquid from the basin when the room’s resident had finished washing. Such equipment allowed the Washingtons and their guests to maintain the cleanliness of face and hands that was indicative of gentility.

    Purchase, 1953 [M-1977/B]
