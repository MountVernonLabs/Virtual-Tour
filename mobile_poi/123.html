• Large Looking Glass (1 of 3)

    This towering looking glass (nearly seven feet high) caught President George Washington’s eye while at the handsome New York mansion of the Comte de Moustier, the French envoy to the United States. After Moustier was recalled to France in October 1789, Washington rented his former residence and purchased a sizable quantity of his furnishings, including this glass and its mate. The pair subsequently hung in the green drawing room of the Philadelphia executive residence.

  • Large Looking Glass (2 of 3)

    The narrow, gilt molding that frames this looking glass and its unadorned, rectilinear profile are hallmarks of the Neoclassical taste fashionable in late 18th-century America. Both elements contrast strongly with the dark wood, scrolled pediments, and elaborate carving seen on earlier looking glasses. Washington’s original looking glass hangs on the river side of the New Room’s Palladian window, balanced by a 20th-century reproduction.
