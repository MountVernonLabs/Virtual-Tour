• Washington’s Potomac Vision (1 of 2)

    In addition to offering a beautiful vista, the Potomac River figured prominently in Washington’s continental vision for America’s future.  Washington had travelled up the Potomac and over the Allegheny Mountains and knew that the headwaters of the Ohio River lay in close proximity to those of the Potomac.  Inspired by the work of British engineers in creating inland waterways, Washington believed that a navigable route could be created between the two rivers to link settlers in the interior of the continent with markets on the eastern seaboard.

  • An Artery of Commerce (2 of 2)

    Hoping to establish the Potomac River as the primary east-west commercial artery, Washington helped found the Potomac Company in 1785.  The major project of the company was the creation of a canal that would skirt the Great Falls of the Potomac. Completed in 1802, after Washington’s death, the Potomac Canal increased trade between eastern and western Maryland and Virginia, with boats carrying products such as flour, whiskey, pig iron, pork, beef, tobacco, and even cast iron stoves. In 1828, it was taken over by the Chesapeake and Ohio Company.
