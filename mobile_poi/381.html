• Mantel (1 of 2)

    This chimney piece, as mantels were called in the 18th century, is the most elaborate on the second floor indicating that this room was the most important bedroom. It is a simplified version of ‘chimney piece C’ in plate LXV of Batty Langley’s The City and Country Builder's and Workman's Treasury of Designs (1756).

    Image credit: Digital Library for Decorative Arts and Material Culture, University of Wisconsin Digital Collections Center
  • Mantel (2 of 2)

    Plinth blocks on the floor support flat console buttresses upholding the border that surrounds the stone facing around the firebox. Above this a swelling frieze, known as a bombé frieze, supports the cornice or shelf.
