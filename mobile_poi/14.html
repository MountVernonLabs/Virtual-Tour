• Blue and White Porcelain (1 of 4)

    Then as now, the design and quality of table wares indicated the formality of the meal. For many meals, the Washingtons used the "blew and white china in common use," the Chinese porcelain dinner services purchased in 1762 and 1783. Unlike the formal, matched services acquired by the Washingtons, the blue and white china exhibited a variety of forms and patterns. As such, it was suitable for informal meals.

  • Patty Pan (3 of 4)

    Patty pans—small containers for savory pies, sweet fruit tarts, or puff pastries—came in a variety of materials, from utilitarian tin and copper to elegant Chinese porcelain. This patty pan may have been part of the "1 Dozn China Patty Pans" that Washington acquired in 1762, part of the first known set of blue and white Chinese porcelain used at Mount Vernon.

  • Archaeological Fragments (4 of 4)

    These fragments of a blue and white Chinese porcelain plate were found buried in a trash pit outside the kitchen at Mount Vernon. They likely date to 1775–1800, and are representative of the “blew and white china in common use” at Mount Vernon during the Washingtons’ lifetime.
