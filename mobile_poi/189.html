• The Pig Pen

    Once the distillery was built, farm manager James Anderson, recommended feeding the spent mash—the grain remnants from the fermentation process—to the hogs on Washington’s five farms. In 1799, 150 hogs were penned at the distillery, where they were being fattened for sale. Polish visitor Julian Ursyn Niemcewicz, visiting in 1798, noted that these hogs were “so excessively bulky that they can hardly drag their big bellies on the ground.”
