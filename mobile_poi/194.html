• George Washington (1 of 4)

    The bust shows Washington in 1785, when he was 53 years-old. Washington had only recently returned to Mount Vernon after resigning his commission as Commander-in-Chief of the Army that won the war for Independence.
  • George Washington (2 of 4)

    Painted by Charles Pope Peale in the mid-1780s, this painting depicts Washington as the victorious general at Princeton, his signature achievement early in the war. Even before the end of the Revolution Washington was being hailed as the “father of his country.”
  • Great Falls of the Potomac (3 of 4)

    In 1785, George Washington became the President of the Potomac Navigation Company, a company intended to make the Potomac navigable, to aid in the commercial development of the region, and assure that Western settlers remained tied to the interests of the Eastern States. Washington would hang this landscape by George Beck in his “New Room” in the Mansion House.

    George Beck’s The Passage of the Pato'k thro' the blew mountain, at the confluence of that River with the Shan'h, 1797
  • George Washington (4 of 4)

    George Washington’s bust was created by StudioEIS in 2013. It was based upon the work of Jean-Antoine Houdon, the great French sculptor who visited Mount Vernon in 1785. Houdon’s bust was always considered by his family as the most accurate portrayal.
