• Story: Vegetables for a President

    As Martha Washington was making preparations to come home to Mount Vernon on vacation in 1792, she sent a letter to her niece, Fanny Bassett Washington, who was helping to manage the estate. In the letter, dated July 1, 1792, Mrs. Washington asked Fanny to “…impress it on the gardener to have every thing [sic] in his garden that will be nessary [sic] in the House keeping [sic] way as vegetable[s] is the best part of our living in the country….” Ten days later, on July 11, the Washingtons, along with Martha Washington’s two youngest grandchildren (Nelly and Washy), and all of the president’s secretaries except Tobias Lear, set out from the president’s home in Philadelphia for a few months’ vacation at Mount Vernon.
