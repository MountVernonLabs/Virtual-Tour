• Voorhees Hall, one of five residence halls at The College of Idaho, is a coeducational residence that’s home to 68 students. Living on campus means you’re always just a few minutes away from your classes, recreational facilities, the library and dining hall. More than 90 percent of residents report that living on campus contributes positively to their education experience at the C of I. You’ll meet and develop friendships with people of diverse interests and backgrounds, you’ll have easy access to academic support when you need it, and you’ll enjoy numerous opportunities to get involved in campus activities. Living in Voorhees (built in 1912 and renovated in 1997) or any of the College’s other residence halls gives you the ability to learn beyond the classroom.
