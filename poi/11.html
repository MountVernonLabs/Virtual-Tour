• The Death of General Wolfe

    In 1776 engraver William Woollett executed this engraving after Benjamin West's 1770 celebrated history painting of the death of British General James Wolfe. The central scene depicts the mortally-wounded general in a Christ-like pose after the British defeated the French in the 1759 Battle of the Plains of Abraham in Quebec. Washington's display of this print may harken back to his early career as a Virginia colonel in the French and Indian War.
