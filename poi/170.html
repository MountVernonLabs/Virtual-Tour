• Fan Chair (1 of 2)

    While in Philadelphia attending the Constitutional Convention in 1787, George Washington purchased a fan chair. Its current whereabouts are unknown, but this period example likely resembles the one he owned. Invented in 1786 for artist Charles Willson Peale by John Cram, a Philadelphia "instrument maker", the mechanism consisted of a treadle below the sitter's feet that moved the fan suspended above the chair.

  • Fan Chair (2 of 2)

    Peale hoped the fan chair would be “useful to the studious and others that are obliged to sit at their employments . . . to keep them cool. . . .” Washington installed the chair in his study, most likely for the convenience of the private secretaries who he employed to organize his military and plantation records.
