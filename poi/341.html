• Root Cellar

    Root cellars were common features in slave dwellings, based on archaeological evidence from Mount Vernon and other 18th-century plantations. The pits functioned as storage spaces, often for the preservation of fruits and vegetables. Enslaved residents dug these sub-floor pits into the ground and often lined them with wood planks for stability and sand at the bottom for cushioning the foodstuffs. The radiant heat from the nearby fireplace kept the contents from freezing during winter months.
