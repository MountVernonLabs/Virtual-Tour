• French Buhrstone (1 of 3)

    French buhrstones were were the best type of millstone for grinding wheat into flour. Used in Europe beginning in the 14th century, they were imported into America by the thousands. The French stones were pieces of fresh water quartz bound around the outside with iron bands. Plaster on the back of the stones solidified all the parts into a working millstone.
  • Furrows (2 of 3)

    The grinding face of each millstone is cut with a pattern of grooves called furrows. These furrows work together as the runner stone turns over the stationary bed stone. The corn or wheat passes between the millstones, where the furrows are like scissors, cutting and grinding the grains into meal or flour. Every month or so the miller had to sharpen or dress the millstone, which required re-cutting the grooves or furrows into the grinding face.
  • Mill Bill (3 of 3)

    A mill bill is a double-sided chisel used for dressing or sharpening the grinding surfaces of a millstone.
