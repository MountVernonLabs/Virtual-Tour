• Porcelain Vases (1 of 2)

    English merchant Samuel Vaughan presented three “exceedingly handsome” vases, also called “garniture,” to George Washington in 1786, to crown the Italian marble mantelpiece that had arrived from Vaughan the year prior. Vaughan had advised Washington on his choice of furnishings for the New Room, and his generous gift of the vases and mantel gave the room an air of cosmopolitan sophistication.

  • Lions, Tigers, and Leopards (2 of 2)

    Jeffereys Hammett O’Neale, an Irish miniaturist, painted the central scenes on each vase. He based his depictions of the great cats—lions, tigers, and leopards—on the earlier work of illustrator Francis Barlow.
