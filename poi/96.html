• Custine cup and saucer (1 of 2)

    At Martha Washington's invitation, the Comte de Custine de Sarreck, commander of the French Saintonge regiment, and several of his officers dined at Mount Vernon on July 20, 1782. Custine presented Mrs. Washington with this tea and coffee service made at his Niderviller porcelain factory. The center of each piece is decorated with the monogram “GW” on a celestial cloud below a wreath of roses.

  • Custine saucer (2 of 2)

    The border pattern on each piece of the Custine china is distinctive. The combination of gilt scroll and beaded borders on this saucer was known as “No. 21”. At least 12 different patterns are represented on surviving components of the service. Custine may have intended them to be an advertisement of his factory’s products to the new American market.
