• Treasures in the Closet (1 of 4)

    The door in the southwest corner of the room leads to a small closet, where George Washington likely kept many of the treasured mementoes listed in his 1799 inventory. This skillfully decorated presentation sword was sent to him in 1795 by an admiring Prussian swordsmith, Theodore Alte. Its dedication praises Washington as “DESTROYER OF DESPOTISM, PROTECTOR OF FREEDOM.”

    Gift of Alice L. Riggs, Col. E. F. Riggs, the Rev. T. L. Riggs and Jane A. Riggs, Vice Regent for the District of Columbia, 1924 [W-85]
  • The Braddock Sash (2 of 4)

    Another of Washington’s wartime relics is this sprang-woven silk sash, given to him in July 1754 after the disastrous Battle of the Monongahela, by the dying British general Edward Braddock. Sprang-weaving was an ancient technique for making textiles by hand-twisting strands of fibers. It created a lightweight, stretchy sash that was strong enough to be used as a stretcher. Washington may have worn this sash for his 1772 portrait.

    Purchased with funds donated by Prince Yoshihisa Tokugawa, 1918 [W-86]
  • Portable Sundial (3 of 4)

    Made in Paris before 1720, this handy pocket device provided a portable sundial and compass measuring well under three inches across. This miniature marvel is said to have been given to Washington by Benjamin Franklin, who would have had ample time to shop for such delights while living in Paris as an American emissary, 1776–1785. The small bird folds flat when not in use as a sundial.

    Gift of Katherine Merle-Smith Thomas, 2010 [W-4907]
  • Bronze Die (4 of 4)

    This bronze die was intended to be used to impress a wax seal on official documents. It is believed to be listed in George Washington’s inventory as “1 Brass engraving of the Arms of the U. States.” Its design closely resembles the Great Seal of the United States, and it may have been created as a presidential seal (although Washington is not known to have used such a device).

    Credit line: Gift of Ellen Winchester Dorsett Husmann, 1945 [H-1210]
