• Thomas Jefferson (1 of 3)

    The bust shows Jefferson in the year 1785, when he was 42 years old, the year Jefferson succeeded Benjamin Franklin as the minister Plenipotentiary of the United States to France, and the year his Notes on the State of Virginia were first published. He would become the third President of the United States in 1801.
  • Thomas Jefferson (2 of 3)

    Jefferson would be deeply affected by his time in Paris, and would become involved in the initial stirrings of the French Revolution. In 1785 he corresponded with Washington on numerous matters including the future danger of the Society of the Cincinnati, the Potomac Navigation Company, and the state of U.S. foreign affairs.”
  • Thomas Jefferson (3 of 3)

    Thomas Jefferson’s bust was created by StudioEIS in 2013 and based upon period paintings as well as the bust of Jefferson done by Jean-Antoine Houdon by in 1789, while Jefferson lived in Paris as the Minister Plenipotentiary of the United States to France.
