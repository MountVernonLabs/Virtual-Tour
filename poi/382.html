• Closet (1 of 3)

    The closet entered through the door in the south wall was constructed in 1775, when the private south wing of the house was built. The interior of the closet is exceptionally well preserved, featuring wooden shelves that wrap around three of the walls. The shelves are supported on brackets with an ogee, or wave-like, profile, a testimony to the care given to design details in the 18th century. High on the south wall of the closet is a peg rack for hanging garments.
  • Discovering an Old Door (2 of 3)

    Infrared photography has revealed the existence of an earlier door through the south wall that predates the building of the 1775 closet. This door would have led to the roof of a one-story addition added in 1758, along with a similar addition on the north side of the house.
  • Closet (3 of 3)

    These additions were also called to as ‘closets’, small rooms normally used privacy. Evidence for a similar door is visible in the blue bedroom. The old closet additions were taken down to construct the two-story wings in the 1770s.
