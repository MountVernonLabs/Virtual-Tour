• Rustication (1 of 2)

    If you look closely at the walls of the Mount Vernon Mansion you will realize that the exterior is made of wood, not stone. George Washington used a technique called rustication to make his wooden house look like it was made of stone. The effect is achieved by cutting and beveling the wooden siding boards (at Mount Vernon the boards are made of pine) at regular intervals to simulate stone blocks, and then applying sand to the painted surface while wet, thus creating a rough, stone-like texture.
  • Rustication (2 of 2)

    In 1796, Washington wrote to his farm manager, James Anderson, with specific instructions on how to proceed with rusticating the Mansion. In his letter, Washington referred to having used beach sand in the past, but he also wanted Anderson to experiment with obtaining the sand by pounding the local sandstone.
