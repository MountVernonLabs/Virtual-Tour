• Trees

    The Children of the American Revolution planted the Southern Magnolia on April 29, 1958. On November 13, 1919, future King Edward VIII, then the Prince of Wales, visited Mount Vernon. Following the tradition set by his grandfather, King Edward VII, who planted a tree during his visit in 1860, the Prince planted the English Yew tree. In 1899, the Mount Vernon Ladies’ Association commemorated the centennial of Washington’s death by planting a Cedar of Lebanon tree near Washington’s tomb.
