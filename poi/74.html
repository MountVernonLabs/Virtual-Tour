• Protecting Washington’s View (1 of 3)

    Most visitors to Mount Vernon, in Washington’s day and today, are captivated by the beautiful view of the Potomac River and the Maryland shore. Protecting Washington’s view from development has required the constant attention of the Mount Vernon Ladies’ Association. Concerned by the planned construction of a tank farm on the far shore in 1955, Frances Payne Bolton, Vice Regent for Ohio, led this effort by purchasing 500 acres of Maryland land.
  • The Kennedys (2 of 3)

    First Lady Jacqueline Kennedy wrote a letter in 1961 raving about the view from Mount Vernon and arguing for its protection. On October 4, 1961, her husband, President John F. Kennedy, authorized the creation of the Piscataway National Park—a park that now encompasses 4,650 acres of land on the Maryland shore. That same year, President and Mrs. Kennedy hosted a state dinner at Mount Vernon honoring Mohammad Ayub Khan, president of Pakistan, pictured here with his daughter, Begum Nasir Akhtar Aurangzeb.

  • The Fight Continues (3 of 3)

    Despite the many hard-won victories toward preserving Washington’s view, numerous challenges remain. Mount Vernon collaborated with the National Park Service, the Conservation Fund, the Trust for Public Land, the Accokeek Foundation, and Maryland neighbors to protect additional acreage. Recently, the Association worked closely with a developer to ensure planting of a tree line to block a new shopping area from being visible from the east lawn.
