• Bell System (1 of 3)

    In the mid-1780s Washington outfitted the Mansion with a house bell system that allowed family members and guests to ring for the assistance of a slave or servant. Pulls in various rooms of the house were connected to wires which attached to bells located at several points on the exterior of the house.
  • Bell System (2 of 3)

    The arrangement allowed for improved communication between elite residents of the house and the laborers in the slave quarters, servants’ hall, kitchen, and outbuildings. It also meant that slaves and servants were always “on call.” Reproductions of the original bells currently hang on the south end of the Mansion.
  • Bell System (3 of 3)

    Although Joachim Ferdinand Richardt painted this view of Mount Vernon’s east front in 1870, evidence of the Mansion’s bell system is still visible. Three bells are affixed to the south end of the house, above a porch that was added by Bushrod Washington in the early 19th century.
