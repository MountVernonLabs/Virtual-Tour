• The Bed (1 of 2)

    George Washington died in this bed on December 14, 1799. The bedstead's tall, slender, turned posts, devoid of any carving, exemplify the neat and plain style the Washingtons favored. During the winter, it was hung with dimity curtains to ensure warmth and privacy. Netting installed in the warmer months kept insects out but permitted cool evening air to flow through. The current hangings are reproductions.

    Gift of George Washington Custis Lee, 1908 [W-194]
