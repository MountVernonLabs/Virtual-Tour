• Settee Bed (1 of 2)

    There is more to this leather-covered couch than meets the eye: it is actually an 18th-century sofa bed. George Washington purchased "1 Settee bed and furniture” for £13 in 1774 from the estate sale of "Belvoir," the home of his neighbors and close friends, George William and Sally Fairfax. The Washingtons undoubtedly used this versatile piece of furniture as an extra guest bed to accommodate Mount Vernon's many visitors.

    Purchased by several Friends of the Collection, 2002 [M-4244]
  • Extra Sleeping Space (2 of 2)

    A bed frame pulls out from the interior of the lower case, and the seat back lifts to form a canopy fitted with curtains that surround the sleeper. Washington's settee bed has not been found, but this 18th-century example demonstrates the appearance and mechanics of the unusual form that once stood in the little parlor.
