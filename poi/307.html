• Blacksmith Shop Archaeology (1 of 3)

    In 1755, a chimney was constructed on the “Smith’s shed” suggesting George Washington was building or repairing a blacksmith shop at this location. In the 19th century, the Mount Vernon blacksmith shop was replaced by an ice house. Subsequent archaeology by Mount Vernon provided the information needed for a faithful reconstruction of the blacksmith shop in 2009.
  • Archaeology (2 of 3)

    These bricks are the foundation of the blacksmith shop’s forge. Archaeological excavations at this site occurred in the 1930s, 1950s, 1960s, 1980s and 2000s, and they discovered the brick foundation of the shop’s forge, the locations of the fence posts for the yard, and evidence for the brick building. These remains were important clues for the building’s reconstruction.

  • Archaeology (3 of 3)

    Hundreds of artifacts were recovered during the archaeological excavations and provide details about the work being done by Nat and George, the enslaved blacksmiths during the 18th century. They repaired guns and manufactured hardware, tools, and utensils for use on the plantation.
