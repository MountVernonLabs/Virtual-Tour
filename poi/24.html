• Bull’s-Eye Window

    In 1778, George Washington added an imposing triangular pediment to the west front of the Mansion. A complex oval or bull’s-eye window at the center draws the eye upward to the pediment and relieves the monotony of the rusticated clapboard. The design of the window is derived from British pattern books, and while one might imagine it illuminates a grand space, it actually opens onto Martha Washington’s “sweet meat closet,” an architectural afterthought on the third floor.
