• The Development of the Mansion (1 of 3)

    George Washington’s dwelling began as a one and one-half story house built in 1735 by his father, Augustine, and received its well-known name during the ownership of George’s half-brother Lawrence Washington.
  • The Development of the Mansion (2 of 3)

    George Washington expanded the Mount Vernon Mansion as part of his ascent through Virginia society. He raised the one-and-a-half story 1735 house to a full two-and-a-half story building in 1758, installed the elaborate walnut stair, and added “closets” (one story wings) on the north and south ends. He rusticated the wood siding to make it resemble cut stone.
  • The Development of the Mansion (3 of 3)

    Starting in 1777, Washington added the pediment to the west façade and the famous piazza was also created. In 1783 Washington began construction of the iconic cupola that sits atop the Mansion. In 1785 Washington completed the interior of the New Room and in 1787 he added the Dove of Peace weathervane atop the cupola.
